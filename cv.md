## Professional Experience

### Avassa Systems — Co-founder & CTO

*March 2020 – Present · Stockholm, Sweden*

Co-founded Avassa to address the challenge of deploying and operating containerised applications at the distributed edge — a segment underserved by hyperscale cloud platforms. As CTO, responsible for overall technology vision, product architecture, and engineering leadership.

- Designed and built a distributed edge control plane enabling enterprises and operators to deploy and manage applications across hundreds of edge locations from a single control point.
- Established the company's technical brand through speaking engagements, published content, and industry presence in the edge computing space.
- Recruited and led a cross-functional engineering organisation across distributed teams.

### Cisco — Senior Director, Product Management

*August 2014 – March 2020 · Stockholm, Sweden*

Joined Cisco as part of the acquisition of Tail-f Systems, taking a senior leadership role within the Service Provider Business Unit. Led product management strategy for Cisco's Network Services Orchestrator (NSO) — a market-leading network automation platform with 150 major brand customers worldwide and a $100M annual revenue contribution.

- Led a team of 6 product managers and development community leaders, reporting to the VP of Network Automation and Orchestration.
- Drove the NSO product roadmap through a period of major industry transition towards SDN, NFV, and network programmability.
- Led product strategy across Cisco's global service provider customer base, spanning orchestration of current and next-generation virtualised networks.
- Collaborated with engineering, sales, and field teams across multiple geographies to align product direction with market requirements.

### Tail-f Systems AB — VP Technology / COO / VP Engineering

*June 2006 – August 2014 (8 years 3 months)*

Joined as the third employee in 2006 and held successive senior executive roles across the company's entire history, culminating in Cisco's acquisition at a $175M valuation in 2014.

- **VP Technology / North America CTO** (Feb 2010 – Aug 2014): Led commercialisation of the ConfD (embedded network management framework) and NCS (Network Control System) products globally (5 direct reports). Relocated to Silicon Valley in 2013 as North America CTO to establish US operations and drive strategic partnerships ahead of the exit.
- **COO** (Oct 2007 – Nov 2010): Oversaw operational functions including finance, partnerships, and customer success, transitioning the company from startup mode to market-fit and expansion.
- **VP Engineering** (Jun 2006 – Aug 2008): Built and led the founding engineering organisation (20 direct reports) through early growth, from minimum viable product to a stable team on consistent release schedules.

### ServiceFactory AB — Co-founder, Systems Architect, Product Manager & Director

*November 1999 – May 2006 (6 years 7 months)*

Co-founded ServiceFactory, a developer of customer service and provisioning platforms for internet service providers. Held successive and concurrent roles spanning systems architecture, product management, and company leadership over nearly seven years.

- Designed and implemented the core platform architecture; produced comprehensive technical documentation covering all configuration and operating aspects of the system.
- Progressed to Director with responsibility for product lifecycle management, roadmap development, pricing, and go-to-market execution.

### Telia AB — Senior Network Architect / Network Operations Engineer

*January 1997 – November 1999 (2 years 11 months)*

One of the principal architects of Telia's second-generation internet services platform — an industry first, supporting a wide variety of access types and application services to millions of subscribers, which later became the foundation of ServiceFactory. Also responsible for defining and maintaining the architecture of the European and North American IP transport network, with active participation in IETF and RIPE. As Postmaster of the telia.com domain, operated a massively scalable email fabric serving 750,000 subscribers across three countries, and developed and published best practice guidelines for anti-spam measures in operator networks.
